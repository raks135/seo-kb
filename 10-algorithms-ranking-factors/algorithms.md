## TL;DR
Google's ranking is a composite of hundreds of signals; no single "factor" dominates. Core updates rebalance the system broadly (not per-page penalties). The helpful-content system was folded into core ranking in March 2024. BERT/MUM improved language understanding. E-E-A-T is a quality lens most critical for Your-Money-Your-Life topics.

## Core explanation
Google runs broad **core updates** that improve overall result quality; they are not targeted penalties. The **helpful content** system (announced 2022, integrated into core systems March 2024 per Search Engine Land, S25) rewards people-first content and demotes search-engine-first content. **BERT** (2019, S13) and **MUM** (2021, S14) are language/AI models that help Google match queries to meaning, including multimodal understanding.

## Mechanics / how-to
- Don't "fix" a core update by changing one tag; review content quality holistically (intent, depth, E-E-A-T).
- Align with people-first content principles (S18/S25).
- Strengthen E-E-A-T signals: author entities, citations, first-hand evidence (S9).
- Use the Search Central blog (S15) for official update announcements.

## Worked example / code
No code; conceptual. Recovery pattern after a core update: diagnose which query clusters lost visibility, audit intent match + content depth + E-E-A-T on those pages, improve, then wait for the next update cycle (weeks–months).

## Assumptions & limitations
- Google does not publish a ranking-factor weighting; most "top factors" lists are correlational (S22-type studies).
- Helpful-content signals are not fully disclosed; March 2024 integration means it's no longer a separate, nameable "update" (S25).
- BERT/MUM affect understanding, not a switch you toggle.

## Empirical evidence
- BERT announcement (S13) and MUM (S14) are Google first-party.
- Quality Rater Guidelines (S9) document how human raters assess E-E-A-T/YMYL — a window into quality goals, not the algorithm.
- Search Engine Land's update library (S25) chronicles the March 2024 helpful-content integration.

## Conflicting views
- **"Core updates are penalties."** Google: they're broad re-ranking, not manual actions (S15).
- **"E-E-A-T is a direct ranking factor."** It's assessed via many signals; raters inform the system but don't directly rank (S9).
- **"There's a fixed top-3 ranking factor list."** Contested; studies show correlation, not causation (see Verify tasks).

## Common mistakes
- Panic-editing after every fluctuation (algorithm-update churn).
- Treating rater guidelines as a checklist rather than principles (S9).
- Over-claiming "ranking factors" without citing evidence.
- Ignoring intent match in favor of keyword density.
